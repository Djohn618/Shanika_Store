Our Story

Discover the journey of Shanika Super & Grocery

2014

How It All Started

In 2014, Bandu Kumara John Kothalawalage bought a small 5-perch land in Trincomalee (No. 119) near the roundabout. With the help of his wife and a few family members, he built a humble vegetable shop named Shanika Super‚Äîafter his wife‚Äîusing wood and coconut leaves.

The shop had no electricity or water. To power the store, they used a car battery to light three LED bulbs. Despite many struggles, sleepless nights, and tireless hard work, Bandu Kumara and his wife dedicated themselves to growing their small business.

2014-2019

Growth & Challenges

Every two months, Bandu Kumara traveled to Dambulla, a city known for its vegetable and fruit markets. Over time, he expanded his business by selling groceries such as rice, beans, and sugar. As the shop became well-known among locals, the earnings improved, allowing them to bring in electricity and water.

Determined to make the store permanent, he took a bank loan from People's Bank‚Äîlocated right in front of the shop‚Äîusing his wife's house permit as a guarantee. With this, Shanika Super & Grocery was born, transforming the once-small wooden shop into a thriving local business.

2015-2019

Giving Back to the Community

As the business grew, so did Bandu Kumara's generosity. He didn't just keep the profits for himself‚Äîhe helped those in need by providing food, school supplies, and healthcare assistance. His support extended to various religious communities, including Buddhist, Hindu, Christian, and Muslim organizations.

However, success also brought challenges‚Äînot just friends but also rivals. But through perseverance and kindness, Shanika Super & Grocery continued to thrive.

2019-Present

A Decade of Success & A New Chapter

More than ten years later, the store is not only serving locals but also supplying major companies and government institutions. In 2019, Bandu Kumara moved to Switzerland with his family, passing the business to his wife's brother, Nipuna Sadaruwan Alenthuge.

Under Nipuna's leadership, the store has flourished, becoming more popular, more profitable, and in better condition than ever.
